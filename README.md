# Payment Behavior by Market: A Gender and City-Level Analysis of Retail Transactions

A Power BI analysis of cash, credit card, and e-wallet preferences across Guadalajara, Mexico DF, and Monterrey — segmented by customer gender.

![Dashboard Screenshot](BI%20Dashboard-snapshot.png)
## Overview

Payment behavior isn't uniform across markets — it varies by city and by customer segment in ways that matter for how retailers plan promotions, staffing, and payment infrastructure. This analysis breaks down cash, credit card, and e-wallet sales across three cities, split by male and female customers, to surface where each payment method dominates and why.

## Key Findings

### Male Customer Payment Preferences
- **Guadalajara** leads in cash sales among male customers at **341,358.79**, showing a preference for in-person cash transactions
- **Mexico DF** leads in credit card sales at **305,312.01**, reflecting a more card-oriented customer base
- **Mexico DF** also leads in e-wallet sales at **342,279.58**, marking it as the most digitally-inclined location for male customers

### Female Customer Payment Preferences
- **Guadalajara** is the top-performing store for female customers in both cash (**389,049.08** — the highest value across any gender-payment combination in the analysis) and e-wallet (**339,216.91**), indicating a strong, loyal female customer base across multiple payment methods
- **Monterrey** leads in credit card payments among female customers at **333,753.74**, suggesting a distinct regional preference for card-based transactions

## Business Implications

These patterns suggest a one-size-fits-all payment strategy would underserve at least two of the three markets:
- **Guadalajara's** cash-preferring base may benefit from continued investment in in-person service quality
- **Mexico DF's** digitally-inclined male shoppers point to an opportunity for card-linked promotions and digital payment infrastructure
- **Monterrey's** card-loyal female customers suggest deeper trust in credit-based transactions than in the other markets
